Automn

Overview

I was as a UI/UX designer for Automn, a student led startup. Autumn is a productivity and scheduling app to help students and young professionals spend their time wisely to achieve their goals. Automn allows users to import their calendar into the app and input what projects and goals they want to work on. From there, Automn helps them schedule their free time to work towards their goals. I worked with five other teammates to create an improved information architecture, design library, and prototype for Automn.

The Problem

The pre-existing information architecture was broad and unfocused. Our clients were aiming to create a habit tracker, planner, calendar, and a social app, all with an artificial intelligence chatbot. It wasn’t clear what problem the app was trying to solve. Furthermore, the visual design was cluttered and unappealing to an audience of young professionals.

Goals

- Conduct user research to identify a specific use cases, and redesign the information architecture accordingly.
- Produce wireframes and design a high-fidelity interactive prototype.
- Create a contemporary design library that remains on brand to the “Automn” theme.

User Research

We conducted 12 long-form interviews with potential users to learn about their productivity and scheduling habits. We also provided a short questionnaire for other potential users to evaluate the pre-existing screens and information architecture. From there, we analyzed our results to draw insights about our users.

New Information Architecture

Based on our user research, we shifted our focus towards helping users plan personal projects and schedule sessions to work on them. We traded the chatbot for a “Smart Scheduler” solution that gives users personalized suggestions about when to work on their projects based on their schedule. This is just a small piece of our information architecture diagram. View the rest here.

Wireframes

The app consists of a dashboard, calendar view, and project page. The Automn button is used to add sessions and projects. Projects are organized using a card-based layout.

Visual Design

Our next step was to update the design library while staying true to the Automn brand. We traded the Automn mascot for a more mature and minimalist leaf design. We chose Lato as our primary font, but added a few more fonts for some visual texture. We preserved the fall theme of the color palette, but brightened it up to give the app a sunny, positive, vibe.

Conclusion

Our final design was very different from what was originally planned. Our research showed that users weren't very keen on a chatbot or a social feature, so we decided to focus on personal improvement and development instead. User research was pivotal in this project, because it allowed us to create a product based on what our users value.
